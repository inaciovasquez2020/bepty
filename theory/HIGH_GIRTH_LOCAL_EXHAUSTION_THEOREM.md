# HIGH_GIRTH_LOCAL_EXHAUSTION_THEOREM

Status: OPEN

## Statement

Let G,H be finite bounded-degree graphs.

Assume there exists r >= 1 such that the multiset of rooted radius-r balls in G differs from the multiset of rooted radius-r balls in H.

Then there exists an admissible BEpTy local valuation B_r such that

B_r(G) != B_r(H).

## Scope

This is the canonical first nontrivial theorem target beyond finite normalization.
